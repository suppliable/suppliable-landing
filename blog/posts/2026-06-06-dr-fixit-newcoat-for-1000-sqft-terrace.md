## Why bother with terrace waterproofing?

Chennai's northeast monsoon (October-November) dumps 800-1200 mm of rain in 6 weeks. A bare RCC terrace will hairline-crack within 2-3 years of casting, and those cracks become leak channels for water that ends up dripping through your ceiling 18 months later.

The fix: an elastomeric acrylic coating that:
1. Bridges hairline cracks
2. Reflects UV (slows further cracking)
3. Resists ponding water

**Dr. Fixit Newcoat** is the standard product. ₹320-360/L, 22 sqft/L per coat, 7-year typical service life.

## The math for a 1000 sqft terrace

**Official Newcoat coverage**: 22 sqft per litre per coat. Apply 2 coats (this is non-negotiable — single coat fails within a year).

```
Litres = (Area × Coats) ÷ Coverage
       = (1000 × 2) ÷ 22
       = 2000 ÷ 22
       = 90.9 L
```

Round up to **95 L** to allow for parapet wall coverage (typically extra 100-150 sqft at ~3 ft up the parapets).

## Application — the 7-step procedure

Skipping any of these means the coating fails:

1. **Surface prep**: Sweep terrace, remove loose material, pressure-wash if possible. Let dry 24 hours.
2. **Crack repair**: Any cracks wider than 1 mm — fill with Dr. Fixit Crack-X Paste first. Sand smooth.
3. **Primer coat**: Dilute first coat 30% with water. Apply with brush/roller across the whole terrace.
4. **Drying**: 6-8 hours, then check the surface — no tackiness.
5. **First full coat**: Undiluted Newcoat, brushed or rolled across the full area + 12 inches up parapet walls.
6. **Drying**: 6-8 hours again.
7. **Second full coat**: Applied **perpendicular** to the first coat (if first was N-S, do this one E-W). Critical for sealing pinholes.

Total job time: **2 working days** with proper drying between coats.

## Common mistakes that void warranty

1. **Single coat instead of two.** Will leak by next monsoon.
2. **Parallel coat direction.** Both coats applied N-S means pinholes line up and water finds them.
3. **Applying over damp surface.** Coating doesn't bond. Wait 24+ hours after last rain.
4. **Skipping primer dilution on the first coat.** Adhesion to bare concrete is much weaker.
5. **Forgetting the parapet wall.** Water entry point #1.

## When to also add LW+

Newcoat is the **topcoat** — the visible defence layer. The other half of proper terrace waterproofing is integral waterproofing **inside the concrete itself**, using **Dr. Fixit Pidiproof LW+** added at the mixer when the slab was cast.

If you're building new, do both: LW+ in the slab pour + Newcoat 28 days later. If you're retrofitting an existing terrace that's already leaking, Newcoat alone might not be enough — the leak is coming from inside the concrete. In that case, you may need to cut and patch with Pidifin 2K topical coating before applying Newcoat.
