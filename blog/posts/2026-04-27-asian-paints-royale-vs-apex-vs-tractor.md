Asian Paints sells roughly 200 different paint SKUs in India and the names blur together. The three you'll actually be choosing between for most residential work are **Royale**, **Apex**, and **Tractor Emulsion** — one for each main use case.

This guide breaks down what they're each for, what you should pay, and where each one is the right choice.

## TL;DR — quick recommendation

| Where | Best pick | Cost (per L) | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interior walls — premium finish | **Royale Luxury Emulsion** | ₹339 | 110–130 sqft/L |
| Interior walls — value | **Tractor Emulsion** | ₹190 | 100–120 sqft/L |
| Exterior walls — Chennai climate | **Apex Weatherproof** | ₹420 | 100–110 sqft/L |
| Wood / metal surfaces | Apcolite Enamel (different range) | ₹450 | varies |

## The 3 you need to know

### 1. Royale Luxury Emulsion (interior, premium)

The flagship interior paint. Smooth finish, washable, low VOC. Used for living rooms, master bedrooms, and any interior surface you'll see up close.

- **Available sizes**: 1L, 4L, 10L, 20L
- **Cost**: ₹339/L for the 1L size; cheaper per L in larger packs (around ₹296/L in 10L bucket)
- **Coverage**: 110–130 sqft per litre per coat
- **Drying**: 4 hours touch-dry, 6 hours recoat
- **Sheen options**: Royale Silk (low sheen), Royale Aspira (matte), Royale Shyne (glossy)

When to use it: any interior where you want a premium look and surface durability. Royale handles scrubbing without dulling — important for hallways, kid's rooms, and rentals where walls get marked up.

### 2. Tractor Emulsion (interior, value)

The workhorse. Decent finish, lower cost, mid-tier washability. Used for bedrooms not facing the public, utility rooms, ceilings, and basically any interior where you want clean walls but don't need the Royale finish.

- **Available sizes**: 1L, 4L, 10L, 20L
- **Cost**: ₹190/L (1L size), around ₹165/L in 20L
- **Coverage**: 100–120 sqft per litre per coat
- **Sheen**: matte, slight chalkiness when scrubbed (vs Royale's smooth wipe)

When to use it: large surface areas where you don't want to spend Royale money — basement, store room, large ceilings, kitchen, utility area. Pair Royale on key visible walls with Tractor on the rest.

[See Tractor Emulsion at Suppliable →](/products/painting/)

### 3. Apex Weatherproof Exterior Emulsion (exterior)

The exterior workhorse for Indian climate. UV-resistant, anti-fungal, bridges hairline cracks, repels rain. Mandatory pick for Chennai's monsoon-and-sun double-punch.

- **Available sizes**: 1L, 4L, 10L, 20L
- **Cost**: ₹420/L for the 1L size; around ₹360/L in 20L bucket
- **Coverage**: 100–110 sqft per litre per coat
- **Lifespan**: 7-10 years if applied correctly (vs 3-5 years for cheap exterior paints)

When to use it: every external wall, compound wall, parapets, columns visible from outside, terrace walls.

Variants worth knowing:
- **Apex Ultima** — 12-year warranty, around ₹650/L. Premium upgrade for high-rise / showcase builds.
- **Apex Shyne** — glossy finish for trim work.

## How much paint do I need?

**Quick formula** (per coat):
- Total wall area in sqft ÷ (coverage per litre × 0.9 for wastage)
- Multiply by 2 coats for finished result

**Example: 2BHK with 1200 sqft of interior wall area, painting with Tractor Emulsion (110 sqft/L coverage):**
- Per coat: 1200 ÷ (110 × 0.9) = 12.1 L
- Total for 2 coats: ~24 L

In practice, order **27-28 L** (one 20L + one 10L + a 1L for touch-ups). Always round up — running out mid-paint job is a multi-hour delay while you fetch more. Or order from us — [60-minute delivery →](/) saves the day.

## Primer is not optional

Skipping primer is the #1 reason exterior paint peels in 2-3 years instead of lasting 7-10.

- **For new construction**: prime with [Asian Paints Wall Primer (Water-based)](/products/painting/) — ₹220/L
- **For repaint over existing paint**: still prime, but Apex Exterior Primer works
- **For wood / metal**: use the matching primer (red oxide for metal, wood primer for wood)

One coat of primer plus two coats of finish paint = the universal standard. Two coats of primer for chalky or freshly-plastered surfaces.

## Putty before primer

Walls aren't smooth out of the plaster. You need wall putty to fill the unevenness before primer.

- **Asian Paints TruCare Wall Putty 20 kg** — white cement-based, the standard pick
- **Birla Wall Care Putty 40 kg** — slightly larger pack, similar quality
- Coverage: 20 kg covers about 250 sqft of wall for two thin coats

[See putty options →](/products/painting/)

## The full paint sequence

For a fresh wall, end-to-end:

1. **Wall putty** — 2 coats, sand between
2. **Primer** — 1 coat (2 for chalky walls)
3. **Finish paint** — 2 coats (or 3 for darker colours over white)

Total turn-around: 5-7 days for a 2BHK, mostly drying time.
