> **Heads up**: these are working estimates, not engineering quantities. Your structural engineer's BOM is the authoritative number — always use that for actual orders.

## Cement: the rule of thumb

For a 1000 sqft house with a standard RCC frame:
- **Slab + columns + beams (RCC work)**: 110–130 bags
- **Brickwork mortar**: 25–35 bags
- **Plastering**: 35–45 bags
- **Flooring + finishing**: 10–15 bags
- **Buffer for wastage/error**: 5–10 bags

**Total: 180–220 PPC cement bags**.

Use PPC (Pozzolana Portland Cement) for most residential work — it's slightly cheaper than OPC and gives better long-term strength. Brands we stock: UltraTech, Ramco Supergrade, Dalmia, JSW, Chettinad. All deliver to your Chennai site in 60 min — [see cement options →](/products/).

**Pro tip**: order in batches of 50 bags, not the full 200 upfront. Cement loses strength after 90 days even in sealed bags. Site-stored bags ruined by humidity is the #1 silent cost on Chennai sites.

## TMT steel: the rule of thumb

Standard residential rule: **3.5–4 kg of TMT per square foot of built-up area** for a single-storey home. For G+1, it climbs to 4.5–5 kg/sqft. For G+2 and above, get an engineer's BOM — don't estimate.

For 1000 sqft G+0: 2.5–3.5 tonnes (2500–3500 kg).

**Mix of diameters** (typical):
- 8mm: 25% — stirrups, rings
- 10mm: 20% — slab top, distribution
- 12mm: 30% — slab main, beam top
- 16mm: 15% — beam main, columns
- 20mm: 10% — heavy beams, larger columns

**Brands we stock**: ARS, Isteel, Arun, Tata. Grade Fe 500D minimum, Fe 550D for any earthquake-zone build. [See TMT options →](/products/) or read [our guide to TMT grades](/blog/tmt-steel-grades-explained-fe-500-fe-550-crs/).

**Minimum order**: 500 kg per diameter for direct delivery. Smaller pickups can be combined.

## Blocks vs bricks — pick one, not both

For a 1000 sqft single-storey, you need walling for roughly 2200–2500 sqft of wall area (external + internal walls combined, both sides).

**If AAC blocks**: 2800–3200 pieces of 6" blocks (one block covers about 0.9 sqft of wall surface). Plus 8–10 bags of AAC adhesive ([Ramco Block Fix](/products/) or MYK products).

**If red bricks**: 22000–26000 pieces standard size. Plus extra cement (30–35 bags) for the much thicker mortar joints.

AAC works out 10–15% more upfront but saves on steel, plaster and labour — see [our AAC vs bricks comparison](/blog/aac-blocks-vs-red-bricks-for-chennai-homes-which-should-you-pick/).

## Sand and aggregate

The cement/sand/aggregate ratio for concrete is 1:1.5:3 (PCC) to 1:2:4 (RCC).

For a 1000 sqft house:
- **M-Sand**: 35–45 tonnes (around 60–70% used in plastering and mortar, rest in concrete)
- **P-Sand**: 8–12 tonnes (finer sand, plastering finish coat only)
- **20mm jelly stone**: 25–30 tonnes (RCC structural work)
- **40mm jelly stone**: 10–15 tonnes (foundation PCC, basement filling)

## Plumbing, electrical, paint — order-of-magnitude

Less standardised because it depends on your spec, but ballpark:

- **PVC pipes (Finolex/Prince/Ashirvad)**: ₹30,000–₹50,000 for full house plumbing
- **Electrical (wires, switches, MCBs, conduits)**: ₹70,000–₹1,20,000 depending on quality

## Order timing — when to bring what

| Stage | Order this |
|---|---|
| Foundation | Cement, steel (8mm for stirrups), 40mm jelly, M-sand |
| Plinth + ground floor columns | More cement, 10/12/16mm steel, 20mm jelly |
| Slab | Most cement consumption (1 day's pour), full slab steel, jelly |
| Brickwork | AAC blocks or bricks, AAC adhesive, M-sand |
| Plaster | Cement, P-sand |
| Electrical/plumbing rough-in | Conduit pipes, junction boxes, PVC pipes |
| Tiling | Tile adhesive, grout, spacers |
| Painting | Primer, putty, emulsion |
| Finishing | Bathroom fittings, switches/sockets, light fixtures |
