## The formula

```
Weight per metre = D² ÷ 162  (kg/m, where D is diameter in mm)
```

This is the standard industry formula used by every TMT supplier in India. The 162 is a derived constant from steel density (7,850 kg/m³) and the cross-section area of a circle.

## Standard TMT weight chart

For 12-metre rods (the standard length supplied in India):

| Diameter | Weight per metre | Weight per 12 m rod | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 mm | 0.222 kg/m | 2.67 kg | Stirrups, small ties |
| 8 mm | 0.395 kg/m | 4.74 kg | Slab distribution, stirrups |
| 10 mm | 0.617 kg/m | 7.41 kg | Slab main reinforcement |
| 12 mm | 0.889 kg/m | 10.67 kg | Beams, slab main |
| 16 mm | 1.580 kg/m | 18.96 kg | Columns, beam main |
| 20 mm | 2.469 kg/m | 29.63 kg | Heavy columns, large beams |
| 25 mm | 3.858 kg/m | 46.30 kg | Industrial, foundations |
| 32 mm | 6.321 kg/m | 75.85 kg | Large structures, bridges |

**Rule of thumb to remember**: 8mm rod ≈ 5 kg per 12m, 12mm rod ≈ 10 kg, 16mm rod ≈ 20 kg.

## Worked example 2: G+1 column reinforcement

For a typical G+1 Chennai home, 6 columns at 230 × 450 mm × 20 ft, each needs:

- 6 × 16 mm vertical bars (each 20 ft = 6.1 m)
- 16 mm stirrups @ 200 mm spacing (~100 stirrups × 1.4 m each = 14 m of 8 mm)

Per column:
- 16 mm: 6 × 6.1 m = 36.6 m → 36.6 × 1.58 kg/m = **57.8 kg**
- 8 mm: 14 m × 0.395 = **5.5 kg**

For all 6 columns:
- 16 mm: 347 kg → ~₹21,500
- 8 mm: 33 kg → ~₹2,050

**Total column reinforcement: ~380 kg, ~₹23,550** in steel.

## Worked example 3: Compound wall foundation

100 m compound wall, 9-inch wide × 4 ft deep foundation:

- 4 × 12 mm bars running longitudinally = 400 m of 12 mm
- 8 mm stirrups @ 200 mm = 500 × 1.2 m = 600 m of 8 mm

```
12 mm: 400 × 0.889 = 356 kg
8 mm: 600 × 0.395 = 237 kg
Total: 593 kg
```

Cost at ₹62/kg = **~₹36,800**.
